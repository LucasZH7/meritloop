## Medium Description

MeritLoop is not just a chat assistant. It is designed as a ritual-native AI companion that helps people complete a full devotional loop every day: reminder, chanting, dedication, logging, and reflection.

Version one focuses on local deployment. It can run without a server and still provide scheduled chanting, scripture playback, dedication records, a transparent merit-score system, and nightly spoken summaries. This makes it faster to prototype, more private by default, and easier to adapt to different home setups.

Its most distinctive idea is the merit mechanic. Instead of reducing practice to generic productivity tracking, MeritLoop turns devotional actions such as chanting, reading, sitting, and dedication into a visible practice index that reinforces continuity and emotional connection.

## Long Description

MeritLoop / 念伴 is a local-first Buddhist ritual companion concept for people who want more than a general AI chatbot. Its purpose is not to imitate a monk or claim religious authority, but to become a calm, trustworthy presence in daily practice.

In version one, the experience is fully local:
- Scheduled reminders or automatic ritual start
- Chanting and scripture playback
- Dedication prompts and ritual closure
- Local practice records
- A nightly merit summary
- A transparent score that reflects consistency and completed devotional actions

This creates a product loop that feels both spiritual and practical. The system can live inside a robot, a shrine-like device, a speaker, or an on-device agent running locally.

MeritLoop is also designed for long-term expansion. Future versions can introduce:
- Family sync
- Shared merit boards
- Community chanting rooms
- Temple channels and verified rituals
- Offerings, bookings, and event participation
- Multilingual content for global Buddhist audiences

The product is especially well suited to multilingual localization. English should be the default language, while Chinese, Japanese, Thai, and other Buddhist-region languages can be layered on top depending on deployment context.
